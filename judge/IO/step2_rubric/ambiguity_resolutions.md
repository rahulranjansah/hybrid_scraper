# Ambiguity Resolutions — user answers 2026-04-21

Answered: the four "Q for user" blocks from `ambiguity_report.md`.
Supersedes those questions; changes rolled into `rubric_v1.md`.

### Q1 — JD-conditional rubric?

> **Yes.** Include the JD (Job Description) as an input to the judge.
> The rubric tags themselves stay brief-agnostic (`level_matches_brief`,
> not `is_hr_manager`); the brief text is injected at call time.

Note: "JD" = Job Description, the client-facing role spec that describes
what we're hiring for. For Crocs, the JD is an HR Manager role; the
target-role cluster is HRBP / HR Manager / Senior HR Manager / HR Director
/ Head of HR. No CHRO, no VP, no C-suite.

### Q2 — "just changed jobs" — yellow sometimes?

> **No. Always red.** The one yellow outlier in the data was a mistake.
> Reason: someone who just moved has low probability of moving again.
> This is an approachability rule, not a quality judgment.

### Q3 — trust the score column?

> **Never.** The 0-10 scores in the sheet were auto-generated by the
> Gemini pipeline. They are "slop without thought". Only the cell
> colour and the `Comments` remark are genuine human signal.

Consequences:
- The judge emits only a label, no score.
- `human_score` is dropped from all training/eval features.
- Step 5 metrics are label confusion matrix + green-precision/recall,
  not MAE on score.

### Q4 — short-tenure / age-55+ — red or yellow?

> **All hard red.** Specifically:
> - Short tenure in current role (under ~6 months) → RED
> - Recently joined → RED (same reason as `just_changed_jobs`)
> - Age **56+** → RED (threshold is 56, not 55; Japanese retirement
>   norms and tenure-seniority concerns)

The `age_55_plus` tag in the draft was wrong. Renamed to
`age_56_plus` and moved from YELLOW to RED in `rubric_v1.md`.
